The Sovereignty Shift: small states in a changing order

Wed 26 March 2026 18:00–20:00 De Haagse Hogeschool, Speakers’ Corner

As global power dynamics shift and old certainties give way, smaller European states face a question they can no longer postpone: how much sovereignty are they willing to pool for the sake of security — and at what cost to their independence?

This dialogue brings together voices from political theory, policy, and student activism to work through the tension between alliance and autonomy. Expect disagreement, evidence, and a room that takes the hard questions seriously.

What to expect

  • Three short opening positions, followed by an open, moderated exchange.
  • Audience questions throughout — this is a dialogue, not a lecture.
  • Refreshments and informal discussion afterwards.

The session is free and open to all students in The Hague. Seats are limited, so registration is encouraged.
